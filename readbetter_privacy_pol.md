## Privacy Policy for Read Better

**Effective date:** March 7, 2026

Read Better (“the Extension”) is designed to help users read web content with customizable highlighting and navigation features.

### 1. Data We Collect
Read Better does **not** collect, sell, or share personal data.

The Extension does not run a backend service and does not transmit browsing content to external servers.

### 2. Data Stored Locally
Read Better stores user preferences using Chrome storage (`chrome.storage.sync`), such as:

- activation mode  
- movement mode  
- read unit mode  
- highlight scope and styles  
- colors  
- shortcut preferences  
- motion preferences  

These settings are stored in your browser profile and, if Chrome Sync is enabled, may sync through your Google account across your own signed-in browsers.

### 3. Website Content Access
Read Better runs as a content script on pages where it is enabled to:

- detect readable text sections
- apply visual highlights
- handle keyboard/mouse navigation

This processing occurs locally in your browser. Website text is not sent to us.

### 4. Permissions Used
Read Better requests:

- `storage`: save and load your settings  
- `tabs`: identify the active tab for settings updates and “Report broken website” flow  
- `host_permissions` (`<all_urls>`): operate on web pages you visit so highlighting/navigation can work  

### 5. Third-Party Libraries
Read Better uses the Mozilla Readability library to identify readable article content. This processing is local to your browser session.

### 6. Data Sharing
We do not sell, rent, or share user data with third parties.

### 7. Children’s Privacy
Read Better is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information.

### 8. Changes to This Policy
This privacy policy may be updated from time to time. Updates will be reflected by revising the effective date.
